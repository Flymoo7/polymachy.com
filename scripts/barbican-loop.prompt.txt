A loose painterly fantasy oil painting drifts almost imperceptibly to life in very slow, gentle slow motion. Visible brushstrokes and canvas texture are preserved at all times; a hand-painted matte concept-art look, never photographic. The camera is completely locked: no pan, no zoom, no dolly.

In a castle barbican gateway, four armoured knights with their backs to the viewer hold their ground against a charging enemy. The four knights barely move and they do NOT step, stride, walk, run or advance forward — they do not travel across the ground at all. They stay rooted exactly where they stand, shifting only a little and very slowly: a small slow shift of weight, a slight turn of the shoulders or head, the soft sway of cloaks, hair and weapon tips. The grey-haired old knight on the left barely moves and stays in roughly the same pose throughout; he holds his longsword steadily in his hand, and the sword moves only as much as his hand naturally does — always keeping exactly the same size and shape, never stretching, growing or distorting. The young knight beside him slowly hefts his poleaxe a fraction. The female knight in the centre slowly settles into her stance, keeping a sword in each hand — two swords in total, one in her right hand and one in her left — both staying in her hands the whole time, never disappearing, merging or changing; a third sword is sheathed at her side and stays in its scabbard. On the far right the elf knight keeps his bow drawn and aimed into the distance at the CENTRE of the scene — directly at the great ogre and the mass of the charging horde around the central gateway. He holds that aim toward the centre and only draws the string back the tiniest amount; his bow stays pointed at the distant ogre and horde and must never drop or angle down toward the nearby knights in front of him, and the arrow is never released.

Every weapon keeps exactly the same shape throughout and never transforms. Ahead of the knights, beyond the fallen soldier, the enemy horde charges and advances FORWARD — the whole mass of figures running and surging toward the knights and the viewer, steadily closing the distance and drawing nearer. They are travelling forward across the ground, NOT jumping, hopping or dancing on the spot. At the centre the huge ogre lumbers forward with heavy strides, clearly advancing toward the knights and swinging its arms. The four foreground knights move slowly and gently with small restrained shifts, while the charging horde and the ogre move with much more energy and clear forward momentum. The castle walls, the gate and the sky stay still. Painterly throughout.
