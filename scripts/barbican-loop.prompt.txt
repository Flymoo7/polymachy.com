A loose painterly fantasy oil painting drifts almost imperceptibly to life in very slow, gentle slow motion. Visible brushstrokes and canvas texture are preserved at all times; a hand-painted matte concept-art look, never photographic. The camera is completely locked: no pan, no zoom, no dolly.

In a castle barbican gateway, four armoured knights with their backs to the viewer hold their ground against a charging enemy. The four knights barely move and they do NOT step, stride, walk, run or advance forward — they do not travel across the ground at all. They stay rooted exactly where they stand, shifting only a little and very slowly: a small slow shift of weight, a slight turn of the shoulders or head, the soft sway of cloaks, hair and weapon tips. The grey-haired old knight on the left barely moves and stays in roughly the same pose throughout; he holds his longsword steadily in his hand, and the sword moves only as much as his hand naturally does — always keeping exactly the same size and shape, never stretching, growing or distorting. The young knight beside him slowly hefts his poleaxe a fraction. The female knight in the centre slowly settles into her stance, keeping a sword in each hand — two swords in total, one in her right hand and one in her left — both staying in her hands the whole time, never disappearing, merging or changing; a third sword is sheathed at her side and stays in its scabbard. On the far right the elf knight stays almost completely still, keeping his bow already drawn and aimed exactly as it is in the original painting — pointing up and away into the far distance toward the gateway where the ogre and horde are. He only draws the string back the tiniest amount and holds that same aim; his bow must never swing down or sideways toward the nearby knights beside him, and the arrow is never released.

Every weapon keeps exactly the same shape throughout and never transforms. Ahead of the knights, beyond the fallen soldier, the distant enemy horde presses forward, figures moving toward the knights. The castle walls, the gate and the sky stay still. All motion is slow, small, gentle and continuous, painterly throughout.
