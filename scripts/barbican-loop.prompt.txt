A loose painterly fantasy oil painting comes subtly to life in gentle slow motion. Visible brushstrokes and canvas texture are preserved at all times; a hand-painted matte concept-art look, never photographic. The camera is completely locked: no pan, no zoom, no dolly.

In a castle barbican gateway, four armoured knights with their backs to the viewer surge to defend their position. The grey-haired old knight on the left and the young knight with the poleaxe just right of him both stride forward, taking running steps toward the enemy, their swords and weapons swinging with the movement. The female knight in the centre lunges forward, her sword shifting in her grip and her tabard swaying. On the far right the elf archer draws her bowstring back and lifts the bow, taking aim to loose another arrow.

Cloaks, hair and weapons sway naturally with the motion. Only the four foreground knights move; the distant charging enemy horde, the ogre, the fallen soldier, the castle walls and the drifting smoke stay still. The movement is graceful, restrained and slow, painterly throughout.
