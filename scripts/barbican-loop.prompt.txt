Subtle slow-motion loop of a loose painterly fantasy oil painting. Visible brushstrokes, canvas texture and a soft matte hand-painted concept-art style are preserved at all times. The camera is completely locked: no pan, no zoom, no dolly, no parallax.

Four armoured knights stand in a castle barbican gateway with their backs to the viewer, defending their position. The grey-haired old knight on the left, the young knight with the poleaxe just right of him, and the female knight in the centre each lean forward and take a single small step as they begin to rush toward their attackers, their weapons shifting only slightly in their grips. On the far right the elf knight raises her bow and gently draws the string back, preparing to loose another arrow.

Only the four foreground knights move, with restrained, minimal, gentle motion. Everything else stays completely still: the distant charging enemy horde, the ogre, the fallen soldier, the smoke, the castle walls and the pink dawn sky are frozen. No new characters appear. Painterly, textured, oil-painting look throughout, motion soft and dreamlike.
