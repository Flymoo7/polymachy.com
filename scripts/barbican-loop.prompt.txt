A loose painterly fantasy oil painting comes gently to life in slow motion. Visible brushstrokes and canvas texture are preserved at all times; a hand-painted matte concept-art look, never photographic. The camera is completely locked: no pan, no zoom, no dolly. The stone castle behind the scene — its walls, towers, battlements and the central gateway — is solid, rigid and structurally fixed; it stays perfectly still and stable the entire time and must never bend, flex, warp, wobble, ripple, breathe or distort in any way.

In a castle barbican gateway, four armoured knights with their backs to the viewer hold their ground against a charging enemy. The four knights stay rooted where they stand — they do NOT step, stride, walk or travel across the ground — but they are alive with slow, gentle, continuous movement and are never frozen. The grey-haired old knight on the left slowly shifts his weight and readies his longsword; the sword sways only as much as his hand naturally does, always keeping exactly the same size and shape, never stretching, growing or distorting. The young knight beside him slowly hefts his poleaxe. The female knight in the centre slowly settles into her fighting stance, keeping a sword in each hand — two swords in total, one in her right hand and one in her left — both staying in her hands the whole time, never disappearing, merging or changing; a third sword is sheathed at her side and stays in its scabbard. On the far right the elf knight aims his drawn bow into the distance straight at the CENTRE of the horde — directly at the great ogre by the central gateway — and slowly draws the bowstring back and holds the tension there. The arrow stays nocked on the bowstring and is NEVER loosed, released or fired — no arrow ever flies — and his bow never drops or swings toward the nearby knights in front of him.

Every weapon keeps exactly the same shape throughout and never transforms. Ahead of the knights, beyond the fallen soldier, the enemy horde charges and advances FORWARD — the whole mass of figures running and surging toward the knights and the viewer, steadily closing the distance and drawing nearer; they travel forward across the ground and do NOT jump, hop or dance on the spot. At the centre the huge ogre lumbers forward with heavy strides, clearly advancing and swinging its arms. The knights move slowly and gently while the charging horde and the ogre move with more energy and clear forward momentum. Only the living figures move; the stone castle, its walls, towers, gateway and the sky behind them stay completely still and rigid. Painterly throughout.
